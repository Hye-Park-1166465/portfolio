What keeps me grounded

Technology is complex and changes fast. But the users are always people — and what people really need usually comes from simplicity.

I want to be the person who stays at that balance point: between the complexity of the systems we build, and the simplicity the people using them actually need.

My story in three chapters →
One

The yoga studio

For four years I ran a yoga studio in Korea. Every day: the same rescheduling, the same records, the same customer questions. I remember thinking — "this is exactly what automation is for."

Two

Korean Air, from the inside

Then COVID. I joined Korean Air's International Reservations team and watched a massive CRM transformation happen around me. From the inside I saw two things clearly: how real systems actually change, and that the work I was doing every day was next in line for AI.

Three

Lincoln, and the honest truth

So I came to Lincoln to study Applied Computing. And honestly? I still feel that same crisis — developers can be replaced too. But I'd rather build with the change than stand beside it.

I'm walking through change — not standing on the side of it.
